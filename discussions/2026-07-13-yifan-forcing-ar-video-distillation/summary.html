Forcing Series: The Evolution of AR Video Distillation

How forcing methods align training history, causal structure, and inference-time rollouts to obtain few-step streaming video generators.

Open full presentation

Research-quality prompts

Central claim: the forcing-method lineage progressively aligns training conditions, causal architecture, and inference-time rollouts.
Hidden assumption: a student's own generated history remains informative enough to serve as a useful training distribution.
Failure boundary: very few steps, horizons beyond training, prompt changes, and constrained KV memory amplify appearance and motion drift.
Connection: the topic links long-video generation, real-time control, causal memory, and event-level evaluation.
Concrete experiment: keep backbone and data fixed while separately changing teacher history, student rollout, and causal-teacher initialization.
Discussion seed: after a student leaves the teacher distribution, what signal can still provide a correct and stable causal update?

Open questions

  1. What is the minimal recipe shared across forcing methods?
  2. When does self-rollout supervision become error reinforcement?
  3. Is a separate causal teacher necessary?
  4. Is there a structural conflict between few-step quality and long-horizon stability?
  5. How should immediate quality, motion, and delayed event consistency be evaluated separately?
